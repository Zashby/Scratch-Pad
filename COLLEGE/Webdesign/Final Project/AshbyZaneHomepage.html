Software Engineering: A Primer


Whether you are checking Facebook, writing a document in Microsoft Word, or even playing a game you are using the work of a programming developer. Programming languages allow for application in a wide variety of fields to produce an equally wide variety of digital products, and there are constantly new fields developing as the needs arise. While it may seem overwhelming at first, it should come off as exciting with all the different possibilities for software engineers to develop and apply their skills. This webpage should act as a primer for individuals wanting to learn more as they identify their own path forward.



So many programming languages, so little time

Where to begin



Starting out can be difficult when, after doing a bit of research, you realize just how expansive the field really is. It can be easy to get overwhelmed and begin piecemeal learning about multiple paths without ever actually moving forward on any. The first thing to decide is what you actually want to do. If you know what kind of application you want to build a career out of it can be easy, but if not it might take a bit of research. Find a programming language you really enjoy working in, or projects that really resonate with you. Never be afraid to ask for other opinions either. With the interconnectedness provided by the internet and websites like LinkedIn you have access to dozens of professionals who may be willing to give you an informational interview about where a path might lead.

About the Field

The software engineering field is large, diverse, and constantly expanding to fill new niches which develop. While traditional education is preferred, there are many self taught developers or those who have participated in a coding "bootcamp" which teaches the basic of development. When looking for a career in this field, developing independent projects are one of the best ways to showcase your skills for an employer. Median pay ranges from approximately $60,000 a year to $110,000 year depending on your location and your specific role with many developers making more than the minimum and working remotely.
